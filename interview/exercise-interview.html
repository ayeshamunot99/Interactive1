Prelude


Leona


Bio


My name is Chenyu Zhang, go by Leona. I’m 20 years old. I’m from China. Graphic design major, sophomore at CCA.
I love for 2D art and packaging. I lean towards working on Photoshop, as I like working with images/ pictures
and editing them rather than working with typography, text, etc. In the future, I want to work independently back
home in China, as I feel like I want to make improvements in design aspects there. People aren’t willing to pay as
much for design, and make design there more important and prominent. 


Conversation


1. What is ‘good’ screen-based design according to you?

I prefer doing analogue first and then developing it online on the respective program.


2. How is art-making online different then art-making offline?

Art-making online feels unreal, however, it’s faster and more professional which makes it more perfect. I like that its freer and has a personal touch to it.


3. What do you think is the ‘purpose(s)’ of the web?

I think that it’s a new way of human life. It has become hard to live without it because everyone around us is doing so. 


4. How has your view of the internet / how you experience being online changed over time?

Feels like it’s all so unpredictable and fast. Although I appreciate technology advancement, it feels like there’s no privacy now compared to before. It also lacks reality. 


5. How has your view of the internet / how you experience being online changed over time?

Feels like it’s all so unpredictable and fast. Although I appreciate technology advancement, it feels like there’s no privacy now compared to before. It also lacks reality.


6. How does the web impact graphic design?

Well, for instance we buy more products online nowadays. I believe online shopping is popular, easy and more comfortable now. Graphic design has to follow
people’s lifestyle. Web also impacts graphic design as it is futuristic, for example: animations attached to e-mails are so cute and capturing.


7. Are there any websites with designs that you like?

I always Consider feedback. I make only 50 percent of the changes if I don’t fully agree with the feedback. If I do agree I will make as many changes as I can.
For instance, if a person has a different aesthetic, I will make changes to improve but not 100 percent follow it.
